A Tribute to

Dr. Apj abdul kalam

1931-2015

"Look at the sky. We are not alone. The whole universe is friendly to us and conspires only to give the best to those who dream and work."

Glimpse of Kalam Sir
    Birth: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, British India.

    Education and Career: Kalam studied aerospace engineering and worked with India's civilian space program and military missile development. He played a crucial role in the development of ballistic missile technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

    Presidency: Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known for his inspirational speeches, commitment to education, and his vision for India's development in various fields.

    Legacy: Dr. Kalam was widely respected as a scientist, leader, and motivator. He received several awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

    Writings: Abdul Kalam was an author and motivational speaker. He wrote several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," where he shared his vision for a developed India.
